## White House AI Executive Order Formally Scrapped <a id="white-house-ai-eo-dead"></a>

**Source:** [Semafor](https://www.semafor.com/article/05/21/2026/elon-musk-mark-zuckerberg-derail-trump-ai-order) · [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/21/trump-ai-executive-order-postponed-why) · **Type:** policy · **Time (UTC):** ~2026-05-22

Reporting from Semafor and Axios clarifies that the White House AI executive order noted in the 2026-05-22 digest as "postponed" is now confirmed dead rather than merely delayed. The order would have established a voluntary framework for frontier model developers to submit pre-release models for federal security review up to 90 days before public launch — not a sweeping regulatory mandate. Between Wednesday night and Thursday morning (May 21–22), Elon Musk (xAI), Mark Zuckerberg (Meta), and David Sacks (former White House AISDO) each called Trump directly; the argument that landed centered on economic acceleration concerns championed by the National Economic Council and Vice President's office. Musk has publicly denied initiating the contact; Meta says Zuckerberg spoke to Trump only after the cancellation was already decided.

**Why it matters:** The U.S. federal government now has no active voluntary or mandatory pre-deployment evaluation pathway for frontier AI models, following the lapse of the relevant Biden EO 14110 provisions. State-level legislation — notably Connecticut SB5 (covered 2026-05-20) — remains the only active domestic policy pipeline in the U.S.

## Intuit Cuts 3,000 Jobs (17% of Workforce) Citing AI Restructuring <a id="intuit-layoffs"></a>

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/intuit-to-lay-off-over-3000-employees-to-refocus-on-ai/) · [eWeek](https://www.eweek.com/news/intuit-layoffs-ai-restructuring/) · **Type:** business · **Time (UTC):** 2026-05-20

Intuit — parent of TurboTax, QuickBooks, and Credit Karma — announced the elimination of approximately 3,000 roles, 17% of its global full-time workforce, effective July 31, 2026. The company simultaneously disclosed multi-year agreements with OpenAI and Anthropic to embed personalized tax, accounting, and marketing agents directly into its consumer products and into ChatGPT and Claude. Affected employees receive 16 weeks of base pay plus 2 weeks per year of tenure, and at least 6 months of health coverage. Intuit projects $500 million in annual savings from AI-driven automation, which funds the severance outlay.

**Why it matters:** At 17%, this is among the largest proportional AI-attributed headcount reductions at a major software company in 2026. The explicit accounting — savings fund severance, agent partnerships are named — makes this a more legible data point than typical restructuring announcements for assessing the pace at which AI is displacing knowledge-work roles.

## Jack Clark at Oxford: Nobel Breakthrough in 12 Months, Recursive Self-Improvement 60% Likely by 2028 <a id="jack-clark-oxford"></a>

**Source:** [TechCentral.ie](https://www.techcentral.ie/anthropic-co-founder-predicts-that-ai-will-within-a-year/) · [Anthropic Institute research agenda](https://www.anthropic.com/research/anthropic-institute-agenda) · **Type:** commentary · **Time (UTC):** 2026-05-21

Anthropic co-founder Jack Clark delivered the Cosmos Lecture at the University of Oxford on May 21, making three specific near-term predictions: (1) AI will assist in a Nobel-prize-worthy scientific discovery within 12 months; (2) fully AI-operated companies generating millions of dollars in revenue will exist within 18 months; (3) AI systems will be capable of autonomously designing their own successors with greater than 60% probability by end of 2028. Clark simultaneously maintained there remains "a non-zero chance" of advanced AI causing mass casualties if alignment fails, calling it "important to clearly state that that risk hasn't gone away." The predictions connect to the Anthropic Institute's published research agenda, which focuses on AI self-improvement economics, recursive capability amplification, and threat modeling.

**Why it matters:** The 60% probability for recursive self-improvement by 2028 is the most numerically specific public estimate from a sitting frontier-lab executive; it lands the same week as the OpenAI Erdős disproof (2026-05-23) and DeepMind AlphaProof Nexus results (this digest), providing external calibration for that timeline estimate.
