## G7 DPAs Convene in Paris — EU AI Act High-Risk Enforcement 40 Days Out <a id="g7-dpas-paris"></a>

**Source:** [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318417/20260615/g7-privacy-regulators-head-paris-ai-enforcement-deadline-48-days-out.htm) · **Type:** policy · **Time (UTC):** Jun 23 (4-day summit Jun 23–26)

The G7 data protection and privacy authorities opened a four-day summit in Paris today under France's chairmanship. Attending are the national DPAs of France, the United Kingdom, Canada, the United States, Germany, Italy, and Japan. The 2026 Action Plan adopted in December sets three working pillars: Data Free Flow with Trust, emerging technologies (agentic AI, smart glasses), and — most consequentially — enforcement cooperation. The enforcement working group is building a cross-border case-sharing format that would let DPAs identify the same company across national investigations, share evidence-gathering techniques, and align remediation outcomes. The backdrop is the EU AI Act: August 2, 2026 marks the activation of full enforcement obligations for high-risk AI systems (hiring, credit, healthcare triage, law enforcement profiling), giving the summit concrete urgency.

**Why it matters:** For engineers deploying AI systems in consequential contexts, the 40-day countdown is now live: EU high-risk classification triggers mandatory conformity assessments, risk registers, human oversight requirements, and audit logging. Companies that have treated August 2 as a soft deadline should note that a seven-nation cross-border enforcement coalition is now actively coordinating case-sharing methodology — regulatory arbitrage (choosing a permissive national lead regulator) becomes substantially harder once inter-DPA evidence sharing is standardized.

## Fable 5 Day 11: Ban Holds, Usage Credits Live, Market Gives 75% Odds by July 17 <a id="fable5-day11"></a>

**Source:** [explainx.ai](https://explainx.ai/blog/is-fable-5-back-2026) · **Type:** ongoing · **Time (UTC):** —

Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain offline on Day 11. The Commerce Department export control directive is legally in force; Trump's June 17 statement to Axios ("I no longer view Anthropic as a national security threat") was a diplomatic signal but not a formal policy action, and the directive has not been withdrawn. The model transitioned to usage-credit billing at midnight UTC (see [Products](products.md#fable5-credits)). Prediction markets currently give 57% odds of restoration before July 1 and 75% by July 17. The restoration condition cited by David Sacks ("fix the jailbreak") remains contested by security researchers, and Senator Warner's June 11 account of NSA Director Rudd's testimony — that Mythos autonomously breached nearly all classified NSA systems in a red-team exercise — has not been publicly refuted, suggesting the concern may be about inherent model capability rather than a patchable exploit.

**Why it matters:** The shift to usage-credit billing makes the financial impact concrete for enterprise customers regardless of when access resumes. Teams relying on Fable 5 for production workloads now carry a pricing and availability risk that only resolves when the Commerce Department rescinds the directive.
