## Pope Leo XIV publishes "Magnifica humanitas" — first papal encyclical on AI <a id="magnifica-humanitas"></a>

**Source:** [Vatican News](https://www.vaticannews.va/en/pope/news/2026-05/pope-leo-xiv-first-encyclical-magnifica-humanitas.html) · [OSV News](https://www.osvnews.com/pope-leo-xiv-to-publish-encyclical-on-artificial-intelligence-may-25/) · [NCR Online](https://www.ncronline.org/vatican/vatican-news/pope-leo-present-his-encyclical-ai-alongside-anthropic-co-founder) · **Type:** policy/cultural · **Time (UTC):** 11:30, May 25 (Vatican Synod Hall)

Pope Leo XIV published "Magnifica humanitas" ("Magnificent Humanity") on May 25, the first encyclical letter in Catholic history devoted to artificial intelligence and its impact on human dignity. The document was signed on May 15 — the 135th anniversary of "Rerum Novarum," Leo XIII's foundational 1891 encyclical on labor and capital during the first Industrial Revolution. Key positions in the document:

- AI should serve humanity rather than displace it; the central challenge is "not technological, but anthropological."
- AI simulating human voices, faces, and relational depth risks eroding distinctively human forms of communication.
- Creative industries face the risk of being "dismantled" as AI takes over production of texts, music, and video.
- Education must equip young people to think independently: "if it disappeared tomorrow, you would still know how to think."
- The encyclical is not opposed to AI per se; it seeks to guide it toward the common good.

The presentation broke Vatican precedent: the American-born pope spoke at the document's launch, and Anthropic co-founder and interpretability researcher Christopher Olah appeared as a panelist alongside theologians and cardinals. It is the first time a frontier AI lab executive has shared a stage with a sitting pope at a major Church document release.

**Why it matters:** The Catholic Church's formal doctrinal stance now treats AI as a labor and human-dignity issue at the same level as industrial-era capitalism — with roughly 1.4 billion Catholics in scope. For engineers: the encyclical frames AI-generated creative content and autonomous decision-making as ethical questions, not merely technical ones, in a way that will shape regulatory conversations in Catholic-majority countries and in institutions such as the EU and Latin American governments.

## Anthropic in talks with Microsoft to run Claude on Maia 200 chips <a id="anthropic-maia-200"></a>

**Source:** [CNBC, 2026-05-21](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/anthropic-microsoft-maia-200-ai-chip.html) · [TechTimes, 2026-05-24](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/317072/20260524/anthropic-microsoft-negotiate-maia-200-chip-deal-claude-could-become-custom-silicons-first.htm) · **Type:** infrastructure · **Time (UTC):** first reported May 21

Microsoft and Anthropic are in active negotiations for Anthropic to rent Azure servers running the Maia 200, Microsoft's second-generation custom AI accelerator announced in January 2026. Built on TSMC's 3 nm process with 216 GB HBM3e memory, the chip is already serving OpenAI's GPT-5.2 internally. Microsoft claims >30 % better performance per dollar versus its previous fleet. No contract has been signed; open questions include the proportion of Anthropic's inference traffic that would route to Maia versus existing NVIDIA/AWS Trainium/Google TPU capacity, and whether Anthropic would co-design future Maia generations.

**Why it matters:** If completed, this would be the first time a non-Microsoft model runs in production on Maia 200 hardware, providing external validation for Microsoft's custom-silicon program. For Anthropic, it would diversify a compute portfolio currently dominated by SpaceX Colossus ($1.25 B/month) and AWS ($100 B+ 10-year deal); diversification reduces single-vendor leverage risk as the company scales toward its first operating profit.

| AWS Trainium | 10-year partnership | $100 B+ |
| Google TPU | Investment-linked | $10 B initial commitment |
| Microsoft Maia 200 | **Under negotiation** | TBD |

## Colorado AI Act takes effect June 30 — enforcement countdown begins <a id="colorado-ai-act"></a>

**Source:** [Cooley State AI Laws tracker](https://www.cooley.com/news/insight/2026/2026-04-24-state-ai-laws-where-are-they-now) · **Type:** regulation · **Time (UTC):** —

Colorado's comprehensive AI legislation — the first state law in the US imposing algorithmic-impact and transparency requirements on deployers of "high-risk AI systems" — is scheduled to take effect on June 30, 2026, with no further legislative action expected to delay it. The law covers consequential decisions in employment, education, housing, credit, healthcare, and insurance; it requires impact assessments, public disclosure of AI use, and an appeals mechanism for adversely affected individuals. Enforcement authority sits with the Colorado Attorney General.

**Why it matters:** Teams shipping models or AI-powered decision pipelines to Colorado users have roughly five weeks to complete impact assessments and disclosure copy. Other states with similar bills progressing — Washington, Florida, Virginia, Utah — are watching Colorado's enforcement debut as a template.
