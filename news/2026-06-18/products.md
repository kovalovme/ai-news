## Midjourney Announces Midjourney Scanner — Full-Body Ultrasonic CT <a id="midjourney-medical"></a>

**Source:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-18/ai-startup-midjourney-pivots-to-health-with-ultrasound-machine) · [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/2196998/midjourney-full-body-ultrasonic-scanner/) · [Latent Space — AINews](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-midjourney-medical-scan-your) · [Shacknews](https://www.shacknews.com/article/149705/midjourney-medical-is-a-new-division-from-midjourney) · **Type:** launch · **Time (UTC):** Jun 17 ~17:00

Midjourney revealed a new hardware venture at a San Francisco event: Midjourney Medical, and its flagship product, the Midjourney Scanner. The device is described as "Fullbody Ultrasonic Computational Tomography" — the company claims it is the first new whole-body medical imaging modality in 50 years. The scanner consists of 358,000 ultrasonic transducers arranged in a ring panel; a patient is lowered into the water-filled ring while the transducers simultaneously emit and receive ultrasound, producing approximately 40 GB of data per body slice. The resulting 3D reconstruction achieves ~0.5 mm resolution for soft tissue. Midjourney states current scan time is about 20 minutes, with a target throughput of hundreds of slices in 60 seconds using custom silicon being developed for the third-generation device. The company claims a dozen scanners running at full throughput could collectively exceed the total MRI scan output of all MRI machines on Earth.

The company is self-funded with no external investors. Midjourney plans to open a 25,000 sq ft facility housing 9–10 scanners in San Francisco in 2027, positioned as a wellness spa entry point before seeking FDA clearance for diagnostic indications. A third-generation scanner using custom silicon is targeted for 2028. The long-term goal is 50,000 scanners worldwide by 2031. The announcement reached 641 points on Hacker News within 12 hours.

**Why it matters:** Midjourney — known purely as an AI image-generation service — is publicly pivoting into medical hardware, betting that its AI model expertise for image interpretation transfers to ultrasonic tomographic reconstruction. The self-funded structure and "spa" entry point are unconventional regulatory wedges: body composition falls outside FDA diagnostic device clearance. No peer-reviewed clinical data, sensitivity/specificity figures, or reimbursement model has been published.
