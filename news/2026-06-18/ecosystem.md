## Noam Shazeer Leaves Google to Join OpenAI <a id="shazeer-openai"></a>

**Source:** [Let's Data Science](https://letsdatascience.com/news/noam-shazeer-joins-openai-from-google-d1a5663e) · [Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/google-gemini-colead-noam-shazeer-to-join-openai-93CH-4748961) · [Storyboard18](https://www.storyboard18.com/brand-makers/google-spent-2-7-billion-to-bring-back-noam-shazeer-from-character-ai-gemini-co-lead-now-joins-openai-101475.htm) · **Type:** personnel · **Time (UTC):** Jun 18

Noam Shazeer, vice president of engineering at Google and co-lead of the Gemini model family, announced on June 18 that he will leave Google to join OpenAI. Shazeer co-authored the seminal 2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need," which introduced the Transformer architecture that underlies virtually every major LLM in production today. He left Google in 2021 to co-found Character.AI; Google paid approximately $2.7 billion in 2024 to bring him back and his team back from Character.AI, installing him as VP of Engineering with a central role on Gemini. His specific role at OpenAI has not been announced.

**Why it matters:** The Transformer co-author moving from Google's frontier model team to OpenAI is among the most consequential individual talent moves in AI since DeepMind's founding team joined Google. It signals that Google's $2.7B retention package did not lock in long-term alignment. For OpenAI, adding the architect of the architecture underlying all current LLMs to the team builds on recent large talent acquisitions and strengthens its frontier research bench heading into an IPO-watched period.

## EU Parliament Approves AI Act Omnibus — High-Risk Deadline Pushed 16 Months <a id="eu-ai-act-omnibus"></a>

**Source:** [Gibson Dunn analysis](https://www.gibsondunn.com/eu-ai-act-omnibus-agreement-postponed-high-risk-deadlines-and-other-key-changes/) · [Hogan Lovells — delay agreed](https://www.hoganlovells.com/en/publications/eu-legislators-agree-to-delay-for-highrisk-ai-rules) · [Global Policy Watch](https://www.globalpolicywatch.com/2026/05/eu-ai-act-update-timeline-relief-targeted-simplification-and-new-prohibitions/) · **Type:** policy · **Time (UTC):** Jun 16

The European Parliament voted on June 16 to formally approve the Digital Omnibus on AI, the first amendment set to the EU AI Act since its June 2024 adoption. The key change: obligations applying to high-risk AI systems under Annex III (stand-alone systems in employment, biometrics, critical infrastructure, education, law enforcement, migration, and justice) are postponed from August 2, 2026 to December 2, 2027 — a 16-month delay. For Annex I high-risk systems embedded in regulated products (machinery, medical devices, aviation), the deadline moves from August 2026 to August 2, 2028. The Council is expected to formally adopt and publish the Omnibus in the Official Journal before the original August 2 deadline. The Omnibus also adds a new Article 5 prohibition on AI-generated non-consensual intimate imagery ("nudifiers") and child sexual abuse material.

**Why it matters:** Enterprises with AI systems in employment screening, biometrics, and critical infrastructure now have until December 2027 to achieve full EU AI Act compliance, removing the immediate August 2026 crunch that was driving rushed conformity-assessment spending. The new CSAM/NCII prohibition takes effect immediately upon publication. Transparency obligations (chatbot disclosure, deepfake watermarking) remain on the original August 2, 2026 schedule.

| Category | Original deadline | New deadline |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|
| Annex III high-risk stand-alone (employment, biometrics, etc.) | Aug 2, 2026 | Dec 2, 2027 |
| Annex I high-risk in regulated products | Aug 2, 2026 | Aug 2, 2028 |
| Transparency obligations (chatbots, deepfakes) | Aug 2, 2026 | **Unchanged** |
| GPAI model obligations | Aug 2, 2025 | **Unchanged** |

## VivaTech 2026: NVIDIA GTC Paris Keynote Focuses on AI Factories and European Sovereign AI <a id="vivatech-nvidia-gtc"></a>

**Source:** [TechTimes — VivaTech preview](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318178/20260610/nvidia-gtc-paris-keynote-headlines-vivatech-june-17-20ai-factories-sovereign-ai-robotics-europe.htm) · [NVIDIA at VivaTech](https://www.nvidia.com/en-eu/events/vivatech/) · [TechCrunch — VivaTech context](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/02/why-vivatech-2026-is-the-place-to-see-europes-ai-strategy-take-shape/) · **Type:** event · **Time (UTC):** Jun 17–20

VivaTech 2026 (Paris Expo Porte de Versailles, June 17–20) opened with NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang delivering the GTC Paris keynote as the show's headline address. The keynote is positioned as a progress report on the European AI factory commitments Huang made at VivaTech 2025 — more than 20 AI factories, gigawatt-class gigafactories in France, Italy, Germany, and the UK. The 2026 framing centers on AI factories, agentic AI, and physical AI as the drivers of a "new industrial revolution" in Europe. The conference draws 100,000+ attendees and features sessions on sovereign AI, defense, cybersecurity, and energy — a markedly geopolitical agenda compared to prior consumer-flavored editions. Notable speakers include Yann LeCun (Meta), Shantanu Narayen (Adobe), and Bernard Arnault (LVMH).

**Why it matters:** VivaTech has become the main public venue for NVIDIA's European sovereign AI commitments, held against a backdrop of EU export-control negotiations over Fable 5 and the "trusted partners" framework discussed at G7 Évian last week. Huang's progress report on 20+ AI factories lands as the EU is finalizing its AI governance framework and European governments are accelerating domestic compute investment.
