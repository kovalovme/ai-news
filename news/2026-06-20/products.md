## Google Home Speaker with Gemini: first Google speaker in six years <a id="google-home-speaker-gemini"></a>

**Source:** [Google Blog](https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/devices/google-nest/google-home-speaker-gemini-features/) · [Eastern Herald](https://easternherald.com/2026/06/18/google-gemini-home-speaker-launch-2026/) · [Digital Trends](https://www.digitaltrends.com/home-theater/googles-new-99-home-speaker-packs-360-degree-audio-and-lets-you-talk-to-gemini/) · **Type:** launch · **Time (UTC):** Jun 17–18 (pre-order live; ships Jun 25)

Google announced the Google Home Speaker — $99.99, available June 25 in four colors (Hazel, Porcelain, Jade, Berry) — its first new smart speaker since 2020. Unlike prior Google Nest/Home devices built around Google Assistant, this hardware was designed ground-up for Gemini for Home. Key capabilities: multi-step natural-language commands ("turn off everything except the bedside lamp"), Continued Conversation (microphone stays hot for follow-ups), Gemini Live for extended voice conversations, Home Briefs (household activity summaries), 360° audio, and pairing with Google TV Streamer for surround sound. The device uses a 3D-knit textile exterior; a light ring indicates listening/thinking/responding states.

**Why it matters:** Google is betting that Gemini's reasoning capability can finally close the gap between smart speaker promises and smart speaker reality. The $99 entry price and Gemini Live integration position it directly against Amazon Echo's updated Alexa+ tier.

## Anthropic updates privacy policy to enable Fable 5 identity verification; refund cutoff today <a id="anthropic-id-verification"></a>

**Source:** [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/ai-and-ml/2026/06/15/anthropic-reserves-right-to-check-id-for-claude-subs/) · [Computerworld](https://www.computerworld.com/article/4185515/anthropics-new-privacy-policy-offers-us-consumers-a-way-around-fable-ban-2.html) · [Cybernews](https://cybernews.com/ai-news/anthropic-privacy-policy-id-verification/) · **Type:** update · **Time (UTC):** Jun 15 (published) / Jul 8 (effective)

Anthropic published a revised privacy policy effective July 8, 2026, adding language permitting collection of government-issued ID documents, date of birth, video recordings, user images, and facial biometric templates "if required by the selected verification method." The policy states Anthropic may verify age or identity to keep services "safe and secure" without specifying what triggers a check. Reading it alongside the ongoing Fable 5 export-control order, analysts interpret the update as a legal framework enabling Anthropic to serve Fable 5 to verified US citizens while the directive nominally remains in force. Separately, June 20 is the hard deadline for Fable 5 credit refund processing for affected subscribers.

**Why it matters:** If identity verification allows Anthropic to restore Fable 5 access for US users while still complying with the export control directive, it resolves the most damaging commercial impact of the ban without requiring formal regulatory relief — though biometric data collection at a major AI provider introduces its own civil-liberties concerns.
