# Ecosystem — 2026-06-28

## Fable 5 Day 16: GLM-5.2 Cyber-Benchmark Challenge Enters Policy Discourse <a id="fable5-day16"></a>

**Source:** [Anthropic statement](https://www.anthropic.com/news/fable-mythos-access) · [Semgrep blog](https://semgrep.dev/blog/2026/we-have-mythos-at-home-glm-52-beats-claude-in-our-cyber-benchmarks/) · **Type:** ongoing · **Time (UTC):** Jun 28

Fable 5 remains fully suspended for general access on Day 16 of the export control directive, with Mythos 5 restricted to the ~100 US critical-infrastructure organizations listed in Commerce Secretary Lutnick's June 26 Annex A letter. No restoration date has been announced; the next structural milestone is August 1 (60-day Executive Order framework deadline).

The day's most consequential new data point comes from security firm Semgrep's June 22 evaluation (now circulating widely in policy and infosec circles): GLM-5.2 — Zhipu AI's open-weight model released June 13 under an MIT-compatible license — scored 39% F1 on IDOR detection in Semgrep's harness, outperforming Claude Code (32% F1) by 7 points. The broader claim entering discussion today is that a Chinese lab operating outside US export control jurisdiction has, within 16 days of the Fable 5 ban, released an open-weight model with competitive security-task capabilities available to any actor worldwide.

The policy implication being widely discussed: if an open-weight model outside US jurisdiction achieves parity on the capability class the ban targets, restricting Anthropic's API may protect neither the competitive advantage nor the national security goal that motivated the directive. The August 1 EO deadline and the July 8 Anthropic identity-verification policy remain the next formal milestones.

**Why it matters:** Security practitioners and policymakers are actively debating whether the export control is achieving its objective; an open alternative matching the restricted model on the relevant benchmarks significantly changes the cost-benefit calculation of the directive.
