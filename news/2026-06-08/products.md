## Apple WWDC 2026: Gemini-Powered Siri and Multi-AI Extensions in iOS 27 <a id="apple-wwdc-2026"></a>

**Source:** [Apple Developer](https://developer.apple.com/wwdc26/) · [MacRumors preview](https://www.macrumors.com/guide/wwdc-2026-what-to-expect/) · [RedShark News](https://www.redsharknews.com/apple-wwdc-2026-siri-gemini-ios-27) · [BuildFastWithAI](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-june-8-2026) · **Type:** launch · **Time (UTC):** Jun 8, 17:00

Tim Cook delivered Apple's WWDC 2026 keynote — his final keynote before handing the CEO role to hardware chief John Ternus on September 1 — and announced the most significant Siri overhaul since the assistant launched in 2011. The new Siri is powered by a custom 1.2-trillion-parameter version of Google Gemini; Apple reportedly pays Google $1B per year for the license. The model runs on Apple Private Cloud Compute infrastructure, not Google's own servers, preserving Apple's on-device and PCC privacy architecture.

iOS 27 (and matching macOS 27, iPadOS 27, watchOS 27, tvOS 27, visionOS 27) introduce a new **multi-AI Extensions** system: users can designate ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude as their primary Siri Extension, routing queries to whichever model they prefer. Claude becomes a native iPhone AI option for the first time. A standalone Siri app — analogous in layout to ChatGPT or Claude.ai — supports text and voice conversations with persistent context and automatic history deletion. Developer betas released same day; public betas expected July; OS launch in fall 2026. iPhone 11 and older dropped from iOS 27 support.

**Why it matters:** Apple's hardware distribution means whichever AI model is set as the default Extension is instantly accessible to hundreds of millions of users. For Anthropic, iOS 27 is potentially the largest single distribution event in Claude's history, comparable to the ChatGPT iOS/Android launches that drove OpenAI's consumer growth. For Google, the $1B Siri deal validates Gemini at the consumer scale, though the PCC constraint limits Google's ability to leverage the usage data. Engineers building iOS 27 apps can now invoke Claude or Gemini natively through the Siri Extensions API.
