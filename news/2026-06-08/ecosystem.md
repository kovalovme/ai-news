## AI Lab CEOs Jointly Ask Congress to Mandate Synthetic DNA Screening <a id="ai-ceos-biosecurity-letter"></a>

**Source:** [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/06/05/openai-anthropic-microsoft-ceos-congress-bioweapon-safeguards/) · [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/ai-and-ml/2026/06/04/ai-heavyweights-warn-their-tech-could-erode-barriers-to-bioweapons/) · [Science/AAAS](https://www.science.org/content/article/ai-executives-join-call-stricter-regulation-synthetic-biology) · **Type:** policy · **Time (UTC):** Jun 4–5

The heads of four frontier AI labs — Dario Amodei (Anthropic), Sam Altman (OpenAI), Demis Hassabis (Google DeepMind), and Mustafa Suleyman (Microsoft AI) — signed a joint open letter to the US Congress asking for mandatory screening requirements on synthetic DNA and RNA providers. The letter states that AI is "meaningfully ero[ding]" the knowledge barriers that previously prevented non-expert actors from synthesizing dangerous biological material. The signatories ask Congress to: (1) require that companies selling synthetic genetic material screen all orders against a threat database; (2) mandate record-keeping of orders and customer identities; and (3) formalize these voluntary industry practices into law. Synthetic DNA manufacturers Twist Bioscience and Ansa Biotechnologies co-signed. The letter aligns with the pending Biosecurity Modernization and Innovation Act of 2026 introduced by Senators Tom Cotton (R-AR) and Amy Klobuchar (D-MN) in February.

**Why it matters:** This is the first time the CEOs of all four leading US frontier AI labs have co-signed a joint regulatory request, setting aside competitive dynamics. Coming two months after OpenAI launched Rosalind Biodefense for pandemic preparedness and one month after Anthropic expanded Mythos Preview to critical infrastructure, the letter signals frontier labs are self-escalating biosecurity as their next major safety investment area — with legislative pressure as their preferred forcing mechanism.
