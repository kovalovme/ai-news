## Recursive Superintelligence Exits Stealth with $650M at $4.65B <a id="recursive-superintelligence"></a>

**Source:** [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/recursive-superintelligence-self-improving-ai-funding) · [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/ai-startup-recursive-emerges-from-stealth-with-650-million-to-build-self-improving-ai/) · **Type:** funding · **Time (UTC):** May 13

Recursive Superintelligence, a four-month-old San Francisco startup, emerged from stealth with $650M in funding at a $4.65B valuation. The round was led by Google Ventures and Greycroft, with chip manufacturers NVIDIA and AMD participating. The team of eight co-founders includes Richard Socher (former Salesforce chief scientist, founder of You.com), Yuandong Tian (ex-director of Meta's FAIR lab), Tim Rocktäschel (ex-Google DeepMind), Alexey Dosovitskiy (Vision Transformer co-author), Josh Tobin (ex-OpenAI), Caiming Xiong, Tim Shi, and Jeff Clune. Peter Norvig serves as adviser. The company is committing to build AI systems that can autonomously discover knowledge, continuously optimise themselves, and evolve in an open-ended loop. Its first milestone is a "Level 1" autonomous training system targeting mid-2026, described as having the research capabilities of "50,000 doctors."

**Why it matters:** This is a bet that recursive self-improvement is now tractable as an engineering goal, not a theoretical horizon. With $650M and fewer than 30 employees, the per-researcher capital is extraordinary; the team's collective track record spans the most cited ML papers of the decade. It represents the "neolab" wave — researcher-founded frontier labs structured explicitly around a single high-conviction research thesis.

## Isomorphic Labs Closes $2.1B Series B <a id="isomorphic-labs"></a>

**Source:** [Isomorphic Labs](https://www.isomorphiclabs.com/articles/isomorphic-labs-announces-series-b-investment-round) · [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-12/deepmind-spinout-isomorphic-labs-raises-2-1-billion-to-design-drugs-with-ai) · **Type:** funding · **Time (UTC):** May 12

Isomorphic Labs, the DeepMind spinout that commercialises AlphaFold, raised $2.1B in a Series B led by Thrive Capital, with participation from Alphabet, GV, MGX, Temasek, CapitalG, and the UK Sovereign AI Fund. The round brings total raised to approximately $2.6B. Funds will scale its IsoDDE (Isomorphic Drug Design Engine) and advance its drug candidate pipeline toward clinical trials. The company has active drug design programs with Novartis, Eli Lilly, and Johnson & Johnson.

**Why it matters:** The $2.1B round is among the largest ever for an AI drug discovery company, validating the commercial case for structure-prediction-based drug design at scale. The inclusion of the UK Sovereign AI Fund alongside US and Gulf sovereign capital signals multi-government interest in this specific application layer. For researchers, IsoDDE represents what productising a breakthrough model (AlphaFold) looks like five years out.

## Igor Babuschkin Seeks $1B for River AI <a id="river-ai"></a>

**Source:** [Forbes via Techmeme](https://x.com/Techmeme/status/2055089731520847885) · [Let's Data Science](https://letsdatascience.com/news/igor-babuschkin-seeks-up-to-1-billion-for-river-ai-8c36ce09) · **Type:** funding (in progress) · **Time (UTC):** May 18

Igor Babuschkin, who co-founded xAI and departed in early 2026, is in talks to raise up to $1B at a valuation of up to $5B for River AI, a new research lab incorporated in Nevada on April 20, 2026. General Catalyst is in talks to lead the round; Babuschkin is committing up to $100M of his own capital. The company has fewer than 10 employees at time of reporting. No specific research agenda has been disclosed, though it is described alongside Recursive Superintelligence and a venture from DeepMind alumnus David Silver as part of a wave of researcher-founded "neolabs."

**Why it matters:** Three separate neolabs (Recursive Superintelligence, River AI, and the David Silver venture) are simultaneously seeking large raises at billion-dollar valuations, none yet with a product. The pattern suggests VCs believe the next frontier research breakthroughs will come from focused researcher-led startups rather than incumbents — and that the window for founding at this funding level is open.

## Google I/O 2026 Eve: Gemini 4.0, Android XR, Aluminium OS Expected <a id="google-io-eve"></a>

**Source:** [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/316755/20260517/google-i-o-2026-keynote-opens-tuesday-new-gemini-lands-behind-mythos-gpt-55.htm) · [Android Authority](https://www.androidauthority.com/what-to-expect-from-google-io-2026-3664979/) · **Type:** preview · **Time (UTC):** May 18

The Google I/O 2026 keynote begins May 19 at 10:00 AM PT. Widely expected announcements include a major Gemini model update (variously reported as Gemini 4.0 or Gemini Spark), Android XR smart glasses, and the public debut of Aluminium OS — the long-rumoured ChromeOS-Android merger. Video generation upgrades (Veo 3) and agentic features under the Gemini Intelligence banner are also anticipated. Pre-keynote reporting characterises the new Gemini as roughly competitive with GPT-5.5 and meaningfully below Anthropic's Mythos.

**Why it matters:** I/O is the primary stage for Google to answer competitive pressure from OpenAI's GPT-5.5 and Anthropic's Mythos. For developers, the Gemini API version bump and any announced pricing changes are the most relevant items to watch; Android XR and Aluminium OS matter primarily for teams building end-user applications targeting Google's hardware ecosystem.
