## Claude for Legal: Plugins, Connectors, and Law Firm Adoption <a id="claude-for-legal"></a>

**Source:** [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news) · [Bloomberg Law](https://news.bloomberglaw.com/legal-ops-and-tech/anthropic-pushes-deeper-into-legal-work-with-claude-updates) · [LawNext](https://www.lawnext.com/2026/05/anthropic-goes-all-in-on-legal-releasing-more-than-20-connectors-and-12-practice-area-plugins-for-claude.html) · **Type:** launch · **Time (UTC):** May 12

*(Catch-up: this announcement was made May 12 and was not included in the [May 15 digest](../2026-05-15/README.md).)*

Anthropic expanded Claude for Legal with 12 practice-area plugins and 20+ MCP connectors (see [mcps.md](mcps.md#claude-legal-mcps)). The plugins cover Commercial, Corporate (M&A diligence), Employment, Privacy, Product, Regulatory, AI Governance, IP, and Litigation practice areas, plus plugins for law students and legal clinics. Each plugin starts with a setup interview that encodes a team's playbooks, escalation chains, risk calibration, and house style. Thomson Reuters rebuilt its CoCounsel Legal product on Anthropic's technology. Freshfields, Quinn Emanuel Urquhart & Sullivan, Holland & Knight, and Crosby Legal confirmed live use on active matters. More than 20,000 legal professionals attended Anthropic's most recent legal webinar.

**Why it matters:** The combination of practice-area-specific plugins (which handle domain nuance) with MCP connectors to existing legal software (which provide access to live documents and research) addresses the two main friction points in legal AI deployment. Law firms have historically resisted AI tooling because it couldn't access proprietary matter files; the connector set removes that barrier for the major platforms.

| Plugin Category         | Example Use Cases                              |
|-------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Commercial Legal        | Vendor agreement review                        |
| Corporate Legal         | M&A diligence, closing checklists              |
| Employment Legal        | Severance review, policy audit                 |
| IP Legal                | Patent prosecution, FTO analysis               |
| Litigation Legal        | Discovery prep, brief drafting                 |
| AI Governance Legal     | Regulatory compliance assessments              |

## Meta Avocado Delayed to Mid-2026; Gemini Licensing Floated <a id="meta-avocado-delay"></a>

**Source:** [Trending Topics EU](https://www.trendingtopics.eu/meta-delays-avocado-ai-model-again-might-even-license-gemini-from-google/) · [Quartz](https://qz.com/meta-avocado-delay-mark-zuckerberg-google-gemini) · **Type:** update · **Time (UTC):** May 17–18

Meta's next-generation frontier model, codenamed Avocado, has been delayed again. Internal testing showed performance between Gemini 2.5 and Gemini 3.0, meaningfully short of targets. The model is expected no earlier than mid-2026. More substantively, reports surfaced that Meta is in preliminary discussions to license Google's Gemini to serve as a backbone for some Meta AI products while Avocado development continues — a significant reversal from Meta's traditional open-weights strategy. Avocado is itself expected to launch as a proprietary model, departing from the Llama lineage.

**Why it matters:** If confirmed, a Meta-Gemini licensing deal would mark the end of Meta's position as the primary open-weights frontier-model provider and would hand Google a major commercial win weeks before I/O. For developers building on Llama or planning to use Avocado, the delay and proprietary pivot signal that the open-weights ecosystem may not have a new Meta frontier model until late 2026.
