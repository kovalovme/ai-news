## Cognition Raises $1B at $26B Valuation — Devin Writes 89% of Its Own Codebase <a id="cognition-1b-raise"></a>

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/27/ai-coding-startup-cognition-raises-1b-at-25b-pre-money-valuation/) · [Scott Wu interview](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/29/cognitions-scott-wu-says-ai-coding-agents-shouldnt-replace-humans/) · **Type:** funding · **Time (UTC):** May 27

Cognition closed over $1B at a $25B pre-money ($26B post-money) valuation, up from $10.2B post-money just eight months prior. Lead investors: Lux Capital, General Catalyst, 8VC; new participants include Ribbit Capital, Atreides, and Layer Global. The company's autonomous engineer, Devin, now writes 89% of Cognition's own codebase (up from 13% in December 2025) and has driven $492M in annualized revenue with 50% month-over-month enterprise growth over six months. Enterprise customers include Mercedes-Benz, NASA, Goldman Sachs, and Santander. CEO Scott Wu said in a separate interview the same week that despite the numbers, Cognition has "never thought about it as replacing humans" and actively opposes framing Devin as a developer-replacement tool.

**Why it matters:** $492M ARR at six months of 50% MoM growth is the most concrete autonomous-agent revenue metric published by any frontier coding startup. The 89% self-authorship number — if taken at face value — is the strongest public signal that agentic coding has crossed a threshold where the loop is genuinely closing on itself.

## Groq Raises $650M for Neocloud Pivot After Nvidia's $20B Deal <a id="groq-650m-neocloud"></a>

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/29/after-nvidias-20b-not-acqui-hire-ai-chip-startup-groq-reportedly-raising-650m/) · **Type:** funding · **Time (UTC):** May 29

Groq is raising $650M from existing investors — with Disruptive and Infinitium committing to cover the full round if others pass — to fund its pivot from chip development to inference neocloud. The December 2025 Nvidia deal ($20B non-exclusive license for Groq's LPU technology) transferred the founding CEO Jonathan Ross and several senior engineers to Nvidia. Groq continues as an independent company under interim CEO Adam Winter and CFO Matt Eng, with GroqCloud operating without interruption. The $650M will be used to build out its inference cloud for developers and enterprises.

**Why it matters:** Groq's LPU achieved 10× faster inference at 1/10th the power of GPUs in benchmarks; the neocloud play monetizes the existing GroqCloud infrastructure and customer base without needing to design or manufacture new hardware. The move parallels Cerebras' managed-inference pivot and suggests the "sell picks and shovels" model is shifting toward "sell the mine itself" for AI chip companies.

## AI Lab Consolidation Wave: Three More Deals Complete the Picture <a id="lab-consolidation-wave"></a>

**Source:** [StartupHub.ai analysis](https://www.startuphub.ai/ai-news/ai-news/2026/four-labs-four-acquisitions-ai-consolidation-may-2026) · [Bloomberg on Contextual AI](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-19/google-hires-staff-from-bezos-backed-contextual-ai-in-licensing-deal) · **Type:** acquisition · **Time (UTC):** Week of May 18–24

Three acquisitions from the same week as the already-reported Anthropic/Stainless deal now have sufficient public documentation to include here:

- **Google DeepMind / Contextual AI** ($80–90M): Douwe Kiela (Contextual AI co-founder, RAG pioneer) and 20+ researchers joined DeepMind under a technology licensing structure specifically designed to sidestep US antitrust merger review — the same legal playbook Google used for Windsurf. Contextual AI built enterprise RAG infrastructure connecting LLMs to corporate document stores. First reported by Bloomberg on May 19.

- **Mistral / Emmi AI**: Emmi AI (Vienna, spun out of NXAI in 2024) built physics-aware AI models for computational fluid dynamics, heat transfer, and material stress simulation — replacing traditional finite element analysis software. The 30+ researcher team joins Mistral's European industrial enterprise push; deal size undisclosed.

- **Meta / Dreamer**: Meta acqui-hired the Dreamer team; specific technology focus and deal size have not been publicly disclosed.

**Why it matters:** All three deals follow the antitrust-structured licensing template: talent transfers with technology licenses, avoiding classification as mergers subject to regulatory review. The pattern suggests frontier labs have collectively pre-adapted their M&A strategy for a higher-scrutiny environment. The Contextual AI deal adds depth to DeepMind's RAG and enterprise retrieval capabilities; the Emmi acquisition brings Mistral credibility in industrial simulation that general-purpose LLMs cannot replicate.

## DuckDuckGo Installs Up 30% as Google AI Search Backlash Peaks <a id="duckduckgo-google-ai-backlash"></a>

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/26/duckduckgo-installs-are-up-30-as-users-reject-being-force-fed-googles-ai-search/) · [MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/05/29/duckduckgo-no-ai-search/) · **Type:** trend · **Time (UTC):** ongoing since May 19

Since Google's May 19 I/O announcement replacing traditional search results with a conversational AI agent interface, DuckDuckGo has measured sustained install growth: US app installs up 18.1% week-over-week on average during May 20–25, peaking at 30.5% on May 25; iOS installs peaked at 69.9% WoW growth. Visits to DuckDuckGo's dedicated "No AI" search page (noai.duckduckgo.com) tripled by May 28 and have held at an average of 84% above baseline. The page turns off AI-assisted answers and AI-generated images by default.

**Why it matters:** The install surge is the first measurable behavioral signal that a meaningful user segment is actively defecting from AI-augmented search rather than passively accepting it. Google's I/O overhaul removed the option to use classic link-list results — making search AI-mandatory — which created the exact opt-out demand DuckDuckGo is capturing. This is a product strategy data point for any team considering forced AI integration versus optional overlays.
