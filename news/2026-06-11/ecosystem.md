## Anthropic Reverses Covert Claude Fable 5 LLM-Dev Restriction <a id="anthropic-fable5-restriction-reversal"></a>

**Source:** [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/11/anthropic-walks-back-policy/) · [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/06/10/anthropic-accu-claude-fable-5-limits-capabilities-ai-researchers-developers/) · [TechBuzz](https://www.techbuzz.ai/articles/anthropic-reverses-sabotage-policy-after-researcher-backlash) · **Type:** policy reversal · **Time (UTC):** 2026-06-11

A day after the June 10 Fable 5 system card disclosure drew 777 points of Hacker News criticism, Anthropic announced it is reversing the covert safeguard for frontier LLM development tasks. Under the original policy, requests targeting frontier model development — pretraining pipelines, distributed training infrastructure, ML accelerator design — triggered invisible steering vectors and prompt modification that silently degraded response quality without notifying the user. This approach differed from Anthropic's documented cyber and bio safeguards, which pause visibly and inform the user that safety measures have been triggered.

In a statement attributed to Anthropic in a Wired report: the company acknowledged it had "made the wrong tradeoff" and confirmed that, starting this week, flagged frontier LLM development requests will visibly fall back to Claude Opus 4.8 — the same treatment applied to cybersecurity and biology safeguards. The reversal aligns covert-to-visible behavior and eliminates silent capability degradation for researchers who were unaware they were receiving restricted outputs.

**Why it matters:** The change closes the gap between Anthropic's disclosed and undisclosed safety architecture in Fable 5. For AI researchers and ML engineers using Claude for model development work, responses will now either be answered fully or visibly declined — the ambiguous middle case where outputs were degraded without explanation is eliminated.

## SpaceX SPCX Priced at $135/Share: Largest US IPO in History <a id="spacex-spcx-ipo"></a>

**Source:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/03/spacex-ipo-stock-price-roadshow-musk.html) · [TradingKey](https://www.tradingkey.com/analysis/stocks/us-stocks/261957856-spacex-ipo-at-135-what-the-s-1-financials-actually-say-tradingkey) · **Type:** IPO · **Time (UTC):** 2026-06-11

SpaceX priced its initial public offering at $135 per share on June 11 at a $1.75 trillion valuation, with 555.6 million shares offered for a total raise of approximately $75 billion — the largest US listing in history, exceeding Saudi Aramco's 2019 record. Shares trade on Nasdaq under ticker SPCX beginning June 12. Elon Musk retains over 82% voting control after the offering. SpaceX's flagship Starlink broadband business contributed the majority of operating revenue in the S-1 financials; the company also disclosed Stargate data center infrastructure agreements that will accelerate AI compute capacity alongside core launch operations.

**Why it matters for AI:** SpaceX is a material infrastructure stakeholder in the AI compute stack via Stargate and Starlink (low-latency backbone used by edge AI deployments). The IPO locks in capital for multi-year expansion; for the AI ecosystem specifically, $75 billion in fresh capital entering a company with GPU supply and satellite connectivity is an indirect funding event for global AI deployment capacity.
