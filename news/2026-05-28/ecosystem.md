## China restricts overseas travel for top AI researchers at DeepSeek and Alibaba <a id="china-ai-researcher-travel-restrictions"></a>

**Source:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-26/china-expands-travel-curbs-to-top-ai-talent-at-private-firms) · [Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4596710-china-limits-overseas-travel-for-top-ai-talent-at-alibaba-deepseek-report) · **Type:** regulation/policy · **Time (UTC):** May 26

China is requiring top AI researchers, engineers, and executives at private technology companies — including Alibaba and DeepSeek — to obtain formal government approval before any international travel, according to Bloomberg reporting citing sources familiar with the policy. Designation is based on national technology relevance rather than job title alone. Previously such restrictions were applied only to state-affiliated personnel: university researchers with security clearances, nuclear scientists, and senior executives at state-owned enterprises. Neither Alibaba nor DeepSeek has publicly commented.

**Why it matters:** Extending export-control-style logic from chips and compute to human talent is a significant escalation; it could impede Chinese researchers' ability to present at international conferences, collaborate with foreign institutions, and could accelerate reverse brain-drain as researchers weigh career risk against continued employment at leading Chinese labs.

## Anthropic opens Seoul office, names KiYoung Choi Representative Director <a id="anthropic-seoul-office"></a>

**Source:** [Anthropic newsroom](https://www.anthropic.com/news) · **Type:** expansion · **Time (UTC):** May 26

Anthropic announced the appointment of KiYoung Choi as Representative Director of Anthropic Korea ahead of the opening of its Seoul office. The Korea office is Anthropic's second Asia-Pacific location opened in 2026, following Singapore, and reflects the company's stated strategy of building non-US enterprise infrastructure ahead of a planned October 2026 IPO.

**Why it matters:** South Korea has among the highest per-capita API consumption in Asia; a local presence positions Anthropic to compete directly with Google — which holds long-standing cloud partnerships with Samsung and Kakao — for Korean enterprise contracts in finance, semiconductor, and logistics sectors where Claude's code and reasoning capabilities are in demand.
