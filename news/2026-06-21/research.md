# Research — 2026-06-21

## Reuters Institute Digital News Report 2026: AI chatbot weekly news usage reaches 10% globally <a id="reuters-dnr-2026"></a>

**Source:** [Reuters Institute for the Study of Journalism](https://reutersinstitute.politics.ox.ac.uk/digital-news-report/2026/dnr-executive-summary) · **Type:** survey/report · **Time (UTC):** published Jun 16

The annual Reuters Institute Digital News Report (46 markets, 100,000+ respondents) documents AI chatbot adoption for news consumption in 2026. Weekly AI chatbot news usage rose from 7% to 10% of global adults year-on-year, with under-35s reaching 16%. Growth is geographically concentrated: South Korea, Greece, and Spain roughly doubled usage while the US, UK, France, and Germany showed negligible change. Trust in AI chatbot news answers is 20% globally (6% in the UK) — well below the 37% who trust news overall. Only 4% of AI chatbot news users report clicking through to original sources.

For the first time, social media and video networks surpassed all other categories as the primary news source globally (54% of audiences). Over half of 18–24-year-olds (52%) cite social media, video, and AI as their main news channel.

**Why it matters:** The 4% click-through figure quantifies traffic displacement risk at scale and will anchor publisher licensing arguments with AI platforms. The 20% trust floor suggests the AI news use case is operating at sustained low credibility — useful context when evaluating LLM deployments in news-adjacent products, where hallucination tolerance is effectively zero.
