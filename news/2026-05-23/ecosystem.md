## Nebius Acquires Eigen AI for $643M to Strengthen Inference Platform <a id="nebius-eigen-ai"></a>

**Source:** [Nebius Newsroom](https://nebius.com/newsroom/nebius-agrees-to-acquire-eigen-ai-strengthening-nebius-token-factory-as-a-frontier-inference-platform) · **Type:** acquisition · **Time (UTC):** 2026-05-01 *(catch-up — not in prior digests)*

Nebius agreed to acquire Eigen AI, a Bay Area startup founded by MIT HAN Lab alumni, for approximately $643M in cash and Nebius Class A shares. Eigen AI provides activation-aware weight quantization and full-stack inference optimization for major open-source models including Llama, Qwen, and DeepSeek. After closing, Eigen's technology stack will be integrated into Nebius Token Factory, the company's managed inference platform offering enterprise-grade autoscaling endpoints and fine-tuning pipelines. The founding team will establish a new Nebius engineering hub in the San Francisco Bay Area. NBIS shares rose over 11% on the announcement; the deal is pending antitrust clearance.

**Why it matters:** Inference optimization has become the primary cost lever in production AI deployments; this acquisition gives Nebius a proprietary compression and serving stack rather than relying on open-source quantization tooling. For users of Token Factory, the integration promises lower per-token costs and faster time-to-production for newly released open models.

## SAP to Acquire Prior Labs, Commits €1B+ to European Frontier AI Research <a id="sap-prior-labs"></a>

**Source:** [SAP Newsroom](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-to-acquire-prior-labs-establish-frontier-ai-lab-europe/) · **Type:** acquisition · **Time (UTC):** 2026-05-04 *(catch-up — not in prior digests)*

SAP and Prior Labs announced a definitive acquisition agreement, with SAP committing to invest more than €1 billion over four years to scale Prior Labs into a frontier AI research lab focused on structured enterprise data. Prior Labs, founded by Frank Hutter, Noah Hollmann, and Sauraj Gambhir, created TabPFN — a series of tabular foundation models that set the state-of-the-art on structured-data benchmarks across hundreds of independent academic studies and was published in *Nature*. Prior Labs will continue operating as an independent entity under the SAP umbrella. The deal is expected to close in Q2 or Q3 2026 subject to regulatory approval. SAP also separately agreed to acquire Dremio (data lakehouse) in the same week.

**Why it matters:** Large language models perform poorly on structured business data because they lack robust understanding of tables, numbers, and statistics. SAP is betting that tabular foundation models — trained specifically on row/column data — will become a foundational layer for enterprise AI, rather than having customers rely on fine-tuned LLMs for spreadsheet-scale reasoning.
