## Elastic acquires DeductiveAI for up to $85M <a id="elastic-deductiveai"></a>

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/18/source-elastic-agrees-to-buy-crv-backed-deductiveai-for-up-to-85m/) · **Type:** acquisition · **Time (UTC):** Jun 18

Elastic agreed to acquire DeductiveAI — a CRV-backed AI site reliability engineering (SRE) startup founded in 2023 — for up to $85 million. DeductiveAI raised $7.5M in seed funding, reached approximately $1M in annual recurring revenue, and had been growing more slowly than some competitors. Its technology automatically detects and resolves software bugs and production failures without human intervention, which Elastic intends to embed into its observability and monitoring stack.

**Why it matters:** The AI SRE category is attracting incumbents at pace — the massive influx of AI-generated code into production is creating a matching demand for agents that can monitor and self-heal those systems. For Elastic, the deal is a direct play on the operational burden created by agentic code generation. At $85M on ~$1M ARR, the multiple reflects acqui-hire pricing for both the technology and the team.

## Fable 5 ban: Trump says "going fine," refund cutoff June 20 <a id="fable5-june18-update"></a>

**Source:** [Benzinga / Sacks](https://www.benzinga.com/markets/tech/26/06/53185788/sacks-us-wants-to-lift-anthropic-export-controls-as-soon-as-possible-after-fable-5-fix) · [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318573/20260617/anthropic-fable-5-export-ban-trump-calls-g7-talks-fine-uk-exemption-dies.htm) · **Type:** policy update · **Time (UTC):** Jun 18

Speaking from the sidelines of the G7 summit in Évian-les-Bains, President Trump said on June 18 that negotiations with Anthropic over the Fable 5 export control directive are "going fine" — his first direct public comment on the ban. David Sacks has separately stated the administration wants the ban lifted "as soon as possible" contingent on Anthropic patching the identified vulnerability. As of June 17, both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain offline for foreign nationals. Two consumer deadlines are now approaching: refund processing for Fable 5 credits closes June 20, and the free-trial window closes June 22. The UK exemption proposed during the G7 summit did not advance.

**Why it matters:** The "going fine" comment is the first signal from the executive level that a resolution pathway exists, but no timeline or technical specification for what constitutes an acceptable fix has been published. Engineers building on Fable 5 internationally still have no restoration date.

## Barret Zoph departs OpenAI after five months as enterprise AI sales chief <a id="zoph-departs-openai"></a>

**Source:** [Prism News](https://www.prismnews.com/news/openai-enterprise-ai-sales-chief-barret-zoph-leaves-after) · **Type:** personnel · **Time (UTC):** Jun 19

Barret Zoph left his role as OpenAI's head of enterprise AI sales on June 19, approximately five months after joining the company in January 2026. Zoph, a former OpenAI VP of Research, had joined OpenAI from Thinking Machines Lab (Mira Murati's startup) after being terminated there for alleged unethical conduct. His OpenAI stint was focused on expanding enterprise sales, a priority for the company's 2026 commercial push.

**Why it matters:** The rapid churn at the VP level for OpenAI's enterprise motion is notable; Zoph's departure comes as OpenAI is competing directly with Anthropic and Google for large enterprise AI spend, a market where long-term relationships with consistent senior leadership matter.
