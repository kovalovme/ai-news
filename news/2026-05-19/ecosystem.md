## Elon Musk Loses Lawsuit Against OpenAI and Sam Altman <a id="musk-openai-lawsuit"></a>

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/18/elon-musk-has-lost-his-lawsuit-against-sam-altman-and-openai/) · **Type:** regulatory/legal · **Time (UTC):** May 18, ~19:00

A California jury unanimously ruled against Elon Musk on all three counts of his lawsuit against Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, OpenAI, and Microsoft on May 18. The central claim — that the defendants "stole a charity" by converting OpenAI from a nonprofit to a for-profit structure — was dismissed on statute-of-limitations grounds: the jury found that any alleged harms occurred before the applicable filing deadlines (August 5, 2021; August 5, 2022; and November 14, 2021, depending on count). Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers noted she "was prepared to dismiss on the spot." Musk announced plans to appeal to the Ninth Circuit; OpenAI's attorney described the litigation as "an after-the-fact contrivance."

**Why it matters:** The verdict removes one of the most prominent legal challenges to OpenAI's conversion to a public-benefit corporation structure; with an appeal pending at the Ninth Circuit the question isn't fully closed, but the district-court outcome clears a near-term overhang on OpenAI's planned IPO process.

## Anthropic Enterprise JV: $1.5B with Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs <a id="anthropic-enterprise-jv"></a>

**Source:** [Blackstone press release](https://www.blackstone.com/news/press/anthropic-partners-with-blackstone-hellman-friedman-and-goldman-sachs-to-launch-enterprise-ai-services-firm/) · [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/04/anthropic-goldman-blackstone-ai-venture.html) · **Type:** funding · **Time (UTC):** May 4 *(catch-up: not covered in prior digests)*

On May 4, Anthropic announced a new standalone enterprise AI services firm backed by $1.5 billion in committed capital. Founding partners each contributed $300 million: Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs, plus Anthropic itself. The broader consortium includes Apollo, General Atlantic, Leonard Green, GIC, and Sequoia Capital. The venture embeds Anthropic engineers directly inside mid-sized portfolio companies to redesign core workflows around Claude agents, targeting the same enterprise transformation market as McKinsey and Accenture. The structure mirrors OpenAI's Deployment Company (see below) but operates at roughly one-seventh the scale.

**Why it matters:** Both frontier labs are racing to convert model access into vertically integrated consulting revenue before enterprise spending consolidates around one or two vendors; the PE-consortium distribution model gives Anthropic immediate access to thousands of portfolio companies across the investment partners.

## OpenAI Deployment Company: $4B with TPG, Tomoro Acquisition <a id="openai-deployment-company"></a>

**Source:** [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/openai-launches-the-deployment-company/) · [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/openai-deployment-company-4bn-tpg-tomoro) · **Type:** funding · **Time (UTC):** May 11 *(catch-up: not covered in prior digests)*

OpenAI launched the OpenAI Deployment Company on May 11, a majority-OpenAI-owned subsidiary backed by $4 billion across 19 investors — TPG leading, with Advent, Bain Capital, and Brookfield as co-leads; Goldman Sachs, SoftBank, McKinsey, and Capgemini also participated. Investors are guaranteed a 17.5% annual return over five years. As part of the launch, OpenAI acquired Tomoro, a London-based AI consulting firm (~150 engineers) whose clients include Mattel, Red Bull, Tesco, and Virgin Atlantic. The venture deploys forward-deployed engineers who embed at client sites, identify high-value workflows, and oversee production integration. A subsequent report noted the vehicle may reach $10 billion as additional investors are added.

**Why it matters:** OpenAI's move from model licensing to vertically integrated services — now with a named subsidiary, a guaranteed return structure, and a day-one client roster via Tomoro — is the most direct challenge yet to traditional consulting practices; at $10B the vehicle would rival mid-tier consultancy valuations.
