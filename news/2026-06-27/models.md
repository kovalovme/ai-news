## GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, Luna — Limited Preview <a id="gpt-56-preview"></a>

**Source:** [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/previewing-gpt-5-6-sol/) · [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/2203102/openai-starts-previewing-gpt-56-and-its-three-variants/) · **Type:** release · **Time (UTC):** —

OpenAI began a limited preview of GPT-5.6, a three-variant series, with a small set of trusted partners on June 27. Access was granted at the administration's request, and OpenAI shared the partner list with the US government before launch. A broad public release is planned "in the coming weeks." OpenAI also gave the government an advance preview of the model's capabilities before today's announcement.

| Variant | Role | Input / MTok | Output / MTok |
|---------|------|-------------:|--------------:|
| Sol     | Flagship, "max" reasoning, strongest cybersecurity | $5.00 | $30.00 |
| Terra   | Everyday use, GPT-5.5-comparable perf at half cost | $2.50 | $15.00 |
| Luna    | Lowest cost | $1.00 | $6.00 |

Sol is framed as OpenAI's most capable cybersecurity model. OpenAI spent 700,000 GPU hours on jailbreak research before release and is running a dedicated rapid-response process for newly discovered vulnerabilities — an implicit acknowledgment that offensive-capability coordination is now standard pre-launch practice.

**Why it matters:** The three-tier pricing structure mirrors Anthropic's Haiku/Sonnet/Opus pattern and reflects a market that has settled on speed-cost-quality tiers. More consequentially, the government pre-clearance framework used here means the most capable publicly accessible model was selected by government approval before general availability — a structural change tracked in more detail under [Ecosystem](ecosystem.md#gpt-56-gov-vetting).

## GPT-4.5 Retires from ChatGPT <a id="gpt-45-retirement"></a>

**Source:** [OpenAI Help Center](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/9624314-model-release-notes) · **Type:** update · **Time (UTC):** 00:00

GPT-4.5 was removed from ChatGPT on June 27, completing its announced 30-day sunset period. Existing conversations automatically continue with GPT-5.5; no chat history is lost. The model remains accessible via the OpenAI API with no changes — only the ChatGPT product surface is affected. OpenAI o3 follows, retiring from ChatGPT on August 26.

**Why it matters:** This closes the last GPT-4-era model from ChatGPT, making GPT-5.5 the effective baseline for all consumer users. The API remains stable for developers who depend on GPT-4.5.
