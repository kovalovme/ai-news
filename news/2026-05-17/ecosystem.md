## Anthropic in talks to raise $30B at $900B+ valuation <a id="anthropic-funding-900b"></a>

**Source:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-12/anthropic-in-talks-to-raise-30-billion-at-900-billion-valuation) · [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/30/anthropic-potential-900b-valuation-round-could-happen-within-two-weeks/) · **Type:** funding · **Time (UTC):** Bloomberg reported 12 May; NYT subsequently reported valuation target up to $950B

Anthropic is in active discussions to close at least $30B in a new financing round at a pre-money valuation north of $900B — and potentially as high as $950B according to a New York Times report, which would place it above OpenAI's most recent valuation. Lead investors include Dragoneer, Greenoaks, Sequoia Capital, and Altimeter Capital, with each reportedly committing more than $2B. The round has not closed and no term sheet has been signed. Anthropic's annualised revenue has exceeded $44B, underpinning the valuation. The company is separately exploring a potential IPO as early as October 2026.

**Why it matters:** If completed at $950B, this would be the largest private AI funding round to date and would briefly make Anthropic the highest-valued private AI company ahead of OpenAI. The $44B ARR figure, if accurate, implies Anthropic has transitioned from research lab to large-scale commercial operation in roughly 18 months.

## Cerebras IPO: 68% first-day surge, $95B market cap <a id="cerebras-ipo"></a>

**Source:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/14/cerebras-cbrs-stock-trade-nasdaq-ipo.html) · [Morningstar](https://www.morningstar.com/stocks/why-ai-chip-designer-cerebras-is-2026s-hottest-ipo-yet) · **Type:** funding/IPO · **Time (UTC):** debuted on Nasdaq 14 May

Cerebras Systems (ticker: CBRS) priced its IPO at $185/share on 13 May, raising $5.55B from 30M Class A shares — the largest IPO of 2026 so far. Shares opened at $350 on 14 May and closed up ~68%, pushing market cap to ~$95B. The offering was 20× oversubscribed. Cerebras makes wafer-scale AI accelerator chips designed to challenge Nvidia for model training and inference workloads; it counts Amazon and OpenAI among its partners.

A separate risk flagged by analysts: approximately 86% of Cerebras revenue derives from UAE-based entities — a concentration that CNBC and TechTimes highlighted as an unresolved concern for institutional holders.

**Why it matters:** The blockbuster debut signals that public-market appetite for AI infrastructure hardware remains intense even after the Nvidia run-up, and opens a new comparables benchmark for private AI chip companies. The UAE revenue concentration may weigh on the stock as institutional holders review the S-1 more carefully.

## Frontier AI has broken the open CTF format <a id="ctf-format-dead"></a>

**Source:** [kabir.au](https://kabir.au/blog/the-ctf-scene-is-dead) · **Type:** analysis · **Time (UTC):** — (HN front page today, 375 pts)

Kabir — a multiple-time winner of Australia's largest CTF and a former competitor at international elite level — argues that advanced AI agents have permanently broken competitive Capture the Flag competitions. The core claim: Claude Opus 4.5 can now solve "almost every medium-difficulty challenge, and some hard challenges" autonomously. The effect is that leaderboards increasingly measure *who can afford to run the most agents with the most context for the longest*, not human security skill. The author documents visible collapse signals: CTFTime leaderboard anomalies, legendary teams reducing participation, and prominent community members (TheHackersCrew, Emu Exploit leadership) stepping back.

**Why it matters:** CTF competitions are the primary talent pipeline for security research, red-teaming, and vulnerability disclosure. If the format collapses as a meaningful measure of human skill, the field needs new evaluation mechanisms — and the same dynamic (AI outcompeting humans on well-defined problem sets) is arriving for coding contests, math olympiads (see SU-01 from the May 15 digest), and formal verification benchmarks.
