## OpenAI leans toward delaying IPO to 2027 <a id="openai-ipo-2027"></a>

**Source:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-25/openai-leans-toward-waiting-until-2027-for-ipo-ny-times-says) · **Type:** funding · **Time (UTC):** Jun 25 (still circulating)

Reporting from the New York Times (cited by Bloomberg) indicates that OpenAI is now leaning toward delaying its IPO from late 2026 to sometime in 2027. Two main factors are cited: SpaceX's stock tumbled after its record IPO debut, chilling retail investor appetite for high-profile tech listings; and CEO Sam Altman has reportedly characterized any valuation below $1 trillion as a "non-starter." Advisers gave executives the option of a faster listing at a lower valuation versus waiting for stronger conditions, and the company appears to be choosing to wait. OpenAI filed its S-1 confidentially with the SEC on May 22, 2026, and the deal is being led by Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley. The company is generating $2 billion per month in revenue but is not yet profitable.

**Why it matters:** A 2027 delay removes one near-term structural pressure point for OpenAI's competitive posture — the company won't need to show the clean quarterly revenue curves a public market demands, giving it more latitude to invest in frontier model training and agent infrastructure even if those burn cash. It also reduces the pressure on Anthropic and xAI to accelerate their own IPO timelines.

## Executive Order July 2 deadline: OMB AI vulnerability grant canvass <a id="eo-july2-deadline"></a>

**Source:** [White House EO June 2](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/) · **Type:** policy · **Time (UTC):** Jul 02

The June 2, 2026 Executive Order "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security" contained a July 2 milestone: the Office of Management and Budget was required to canvass federal grant programs for funds that can be directed to developers working on advanced AI vulnerability detection. This canvass is intended to surface near-term Broad Agency Announcement (BAA) opportunities for companies with AI security R&D portfolios. A second EO milestone — the 60-day deadline for a voluntary framework governing pre-release federal access to covered frontier models — falls on August 1, 2026.

**Why it matters:** The grant canvass creates a funding signal for the AI security sector, particularly for companies building automated vulnerability scanning tools (see also Anthropic's Project Glasswing, which discovered 10,000+ high-severity CVEs in weeks). The August 1 milestone will define how the US government intends to access and evaluate frontier models before public release, which could reshape how labs sequence their release pipelines.
