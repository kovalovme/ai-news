## Anthropic Economic Index: "Cadences" report — usage rhythms and worker impact survey <a id="anthropic-economic-index-cadences"></a>

**Source:** [anthropic.com/research/economic-index-june-2026-report](https://www.anthropic.com/research/economic-index-june-2026-report) · **Type:** report · **Time (UTC):** released 2026-06-26 (not previously covered)

Released June 26, this third Anthropic Economic Index report introduces hourly-resolution sampling of Claude conversations and links ~9,700 survey respondents to their actual session logs via Anthropic's CLIO privacy system. The key methodological advance over prior reports is that claimed behavior can be cross-checked against observed usage — surfacing when what users say diverges from what they do.

Key findings: 93% of Claude conversations produce an identifiable artifact (explanation, document/report, guidance, code, etc.); personal conversation share jumps from ~35% on weekdays to ~50% on weekends; tax-related queries spiked 8× on April 14; higher-wage occupations show disproportionate night/weekend usage — consistent with AI extending hours rather than substituting them. On the workforce outlook: 86% reported productivity gains in work speed; 57% felt AI increased their skills' market value; over a third expect AI to handle "most or nearly all" of their work tasks within 12 months; 4% said Claude could perform their entire job today; 10% rated involuntary job loss as "likely or very likely" in the coming year. Paradoxically, the users with the heaviest automatic-mode usage showed *greater* career optimism than those using Claude collaboratively.

**Why it matters:** This is the most rigorous public dataset on how professional AI usage actually distributes across time, task type, and user segment. The 93% artifact rate implies that most Claude usage is producing durable outputs (not just conversation), which has implications for pricing, retention analysis, and estimating AI's economic footprint.

| Finding | Stat |
|---|---|
| Conversations producing an artifact | 93% |
| Respondents expecting AI to handle most tasks within 12 months | >33% |
| Productivity gain reported (speed) | 86% |
| Skills market value increase reported | 57% |
| Tax query spike (April 14 vs average) | 8× |
| Personal conversation share (weekend) | ~50% |

## Claude Code used for MRI second opinion — viral HN moment <a id="claude-code-mri"></a>

**Source:** [antoine.fi](https://antoine.fi) · [HN discussion, 417 pts](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48706942) · **Type:** use case · **Time (UTC):** —

A personal essay describing how a developer asked Claude Code to analyze and explain MRI scan results as a complement to (not substitute for) a radiologist's reading became the second-highest HN story of the day at 417 points. The discussion is notable less for its technical content than for what it signals: high-stakes medical context is now a common Claude use case among engineers, and the community reaction was constructively focused on limits and workflow rather than prohibition. The thread surfaced several real-world patterns — prompting for differential diagnosis framing, feeding DICOM metadata vs. rendered images, and treating outputs as structured second opinions rather than conclusions.

**Why it matters:** Medical use cases were almost exclusively a regulatory and safety discussion twelve months ago; the HN thread's constructive tone suggests the conversation has shifted to "how" rather than "whether." Claude's medical literacy is a product differentiator that Anthropic has not formally marketed.
