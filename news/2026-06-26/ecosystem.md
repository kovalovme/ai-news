## Fable 5 Day 14: Lutnick Deadline Passed, No Public Response <a id="fable5-day14"></a>

**Source:** [explainx.ai](https://explainx.ai/blog/is-fable-5-back-2026) · [Anthropic statement](https://www.anthropic.com/news/fable-mythos-access) · **Type:** update · **Time (UTC):** June 26

Four bipartisan House members sent a formal letter to Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick on June 18 demanding written justification for the Fable 5 / Mythos 5 export controls by today, June 26. The letter posed four pointed questions: Was Anthropic given an opportunity to fix the issue before the ban? Is the claimed capability unique to Fable 5 or common across models? Did Commerce follow required legal process? What is the factual basis for the "military intelligence end use" designation?

As of today, no public response has been issued. Anthropic staff confirmed on June 25 that the company serves exactly zero Fable/Mythos traffic; viral social media reports claiming restored access were attributed to a UI bug in the model picker.

**Next structural milestones:**

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| July 8 | Anthropic's updated privacy policy (with identity verification) takes effect — most likely mechanism for US-first access restoration without formally lifting the directive |
| August 1 | 60-day Executive Order deadline for NSA/Treasury/CISA to complete the "covered frontier model" framework — structural resolution path |

**Why it matters:** A missed response to a bipartisan congressional inquiry is unusual. It suggests either that the legal basis is too sensitive to share in writing, or that the administration has no political incentive to justify the process publicly. Either reading points to August 1 as the earliest realistic structural milestone — and July 8 as a potential early signal if Anthropic begins US-gated access.

## EU AI Act Digital Omnibus: European Parliament Endorsement Defers High-Risk Deadline 16 Months <a id="eu-ai-act-omnibus"></a>

**Source:** [Gibson Dunn](https://www.gibsondunn.com/eu-ai-act-omnibus-agreement-postponed-high-risk-deadlines-and-other-key-changes/) · [EU Council](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/05/07/artificial-intelligence-council-and-parliament-agree-to-simplify-and-streamline-rules/) · [Inside Privacy](https://www.insideprivacy.com/artificial-intelligence/eu-ai-act-update-timeline-relief-targeted-simplification-and-new-prohibitions/) · **Type:** policy · **Time (UTC):** —

The G7 Data Protection Authorities summit closed in Paris today after running June 23–26, coordinating cross-border AI enforcement ahead of the EU AI Act's August 2 activation date. The key backdrop: the European Parliament formally endorsed the Digital Omnibus on AI on June 16; formal Official Journal publication and entry into force is expected in July 2026.

**What the Omnibus changes:**

| Obligation | Previous deadline | New deadline | Δ |
|-----------|------------------|--------------|---|
| Annex III high-risk HRAIS (stand-alone use cases) | Aug 2, 2026 | Dec 2, 2027 | +16 months |
| Annex I high-risk HRAIS (regulated products) | Aug 2, 2026 | Aug 2, 2028 | +24 months |
| Commission GPAI enforcement powers | Aug 2, 2026 | Unchanged | — |

New Article 5 prohibitions (effective December 2, 2026): AI-generated non-consensual intimate imagery; AI-generated child sexual abuse material.

**Why it matters:** Enterprises that had been racing to classify and document high-risk AI systems against an August 2026 deadline gain 16 months of runway for Annex III (use-based) systems. Critically, GPAI provider obligations — which cover frontier model operators — are not deferred and activate August 2 as planned. The Omnibus relief benefits AI deployers more than AI providers.
