## Claude Trusted Devices for Remote Control <a id="claude-trusted-devices"></a>

**Source:** [Anthropic Claude release notes](https://releasebot.io/updates/anthropic/claude) · **Type:** release · **Time (UTC):** June 25

Admins on Claude Team and Enterprise plans can now require members to verify their device before viewing or steering local Claude Code sessions remotely. Once device verification is required, an unverified device is blocked from the Remote Control session view.

**Why it matters:** Remote Control allows admins and authorized users to observe or steer any developer's active Claude Code session — a privileged access surface. Without device verification, a compromised admin credential was sufficient for access. Requiring hardware-tied device verification adds a second factor that meaningfully reduces the blast radius of credential compromise.

## GPT-4.5 Retiring from ChatGPT Tomorrow <a id="gpt-45-retirement"></a>

**Source:** [OpenAI Help Center](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/6825453-chatgpt-release-notes) · [The GPU Trade](https://thegputrade.com/news/openai-retires-gpt-45-and-o3-as-ipo-talk-heats-up-bbsl5asb/) · **Type:** update · **Time (UTC):** effective June 27

GPT-4.5 will be removed from ChatGPT's model selector on June 27, 2026, completing its 30-day sunset. Announced June 3 alongside the GPT-5.5 Instant upgrade, this applies only to the ChatGPT consumer product — the `gpt-4.5-preview` API endpoint continues to function. o3 follows on August 26, 2026 (90-day sunset from June 3).

GPT-5 and GPT-5.3-Codex both exceed GPT-4.5 on standard benchmarks at lower cost per token, framing the retirement as model consolidation rather than capability regression.

**Why it matters:** ChatGPT users with workflows pinned to the GPT-4.5 selector need to choose a replacement by tomorrow. API developers are unaffected for now, but o3's August 26 API retirement date is worth planning for.

## Google DeepMind + A24: $75M AI Filmmaking Partnership <a id="google-deepmind-a24"></a>

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/22/google-deepmind-bets-75m-on-ais-future-in-hollywood-with-a24-deal/) · [Variety](https://variety.com/2026/film/news/google-a24-ai-filmmaking-tools-1236787297/) · **Type:** launch · **Time (UTC):** June 22

Google committed $75 million to A24 in a multi-year, non-exclusive research partnership with Google DeepMind, marking Google's first direct equity stake in a major Hollywood studio. Structure:

- A24 gains access to DeepMind research and compute infrastructure
- DeepMind researchers embed inside A24 productions; near-term focus is AI-generated storyboards
- Google does not gain access to A24's content library or data
- Non-exclusive on both sides: A24 can work with other AI companies; DeepMind can partner with other studios

**Why it matters:** The embedded-researcher model mirrors how AI labs have entered other specialized domains (biotech, materials). Production-embedded researchers build tools against real constraints — real director feedback, real asset pipelines — rather than general-purpose generation. The non-exclusive structure also signals that DeepMind sees Hollywood workflows as an open market rather than a proprietary data moat strategy.
