## arXiv Institutes One-Year Submission Ban for Unchecked AI-Generated Content <a id="arxiv-ai-slop-ban"></a>

**Source:** [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/arxiv-ai-slop-ban-researchers-preprint) · **Type:** policy · **Time (UTC):** — (announced May 17)

arXiv computer science section chair Thomas Dietterich announced on May 17 that authors face a one-year suspension from submitting to arXiv if they post papers containing obvious unchecked AI-generated content. The three specific triggers: hallucinated references that don't correspond to real publications; meta-comments left in the text (e.g. "here is a 200-word summary; would you like me to make any changes?"); and placeholder data with unfulfilled instructions (e.g. "fill in with the real numbers"). The rule is deliberately narrow — it permits responsible AI use for drafting and editing — and applies only when authors demonstrably failed to read their submission. Section chairs must confirm violations before imposing bans; decisions are appealable. After the one-year ban, authors must have papers accepted at a peer-reviewed journal before they can return to arXiv. The policy follows a Lancet study auditing 2.5 million biomedical papers that found fabricated citations have risen twelvefold since 2023.

**Why it matters:** arXiv serves as the primary rapid-dissemination channel for AI and ML research; unchecked AI-generated papers pollute citation graphs and slow down researchers using arXiv as a reference base. The narrow, behavior-targeted enforcement approach (submit-without-reading, not AI-use per se) sets a precedent other preprint servers are likely to follow.
