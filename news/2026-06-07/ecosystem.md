## Anthropic: Claude writes 80% of production code, calls for global pause mechanism <a id="anthropic-80pct-code"></a>

**Source:** [Anthropic Institute](https://www.anthropic.com/institute/recursive-self-improvement) · [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/anthropic-says-80-of-its-new-production-code-is-now-authored-by-claude-how-your-enterprise-can-keep-up) · **Type:** announcement · **Time (UTC):** Jun 4

Anthropic published "When AI builds itself" on June 4, disclosing that in May 2026 Claude authored more than 80% of code merged into Anthropic's own production codebase — up from single-digit percentages before Claude Code's February 2025 launch. Accompanying productivity metrics: typical engineers merged 8× as much code per day in Q2 2026 versus 2024. On open-ended engineering tasks with no clear initial specification, Claude's success rate reached 76% in May 2026 — a 50-percentage-point increase in six months. Quality is described as "roughly at parity" with human-written code today, with Anthropic expecting Claude-written code to be "strictly better" within the year.

The policy section argues the world needs a verifiable cross-lab mechanism to "slow down or temporarily pause" frontier AI development and states Anthropic would exercise such a pause if other frontier developers did so verifiably — framing recursive self-improvement as no longer a theoretical concern.

**Why it matters:** This is the first frontier lab to publish internal AI-authorship metrics at this granularity, and the trajectory (single-digits → 80% in ~16 months) is faster than most published forecasts. The explicit call for a verifiable global pause mechanism — from the lab closest to demonstrating autonomous self-improvement — shifts the policy debate from abstract risk to concrete operating evidence.

## Great American AI Act: bipartisan draft proposes three-year federal preemption of state AI laws <a id="great-american-ai-act"></a>

**Source:** [Obernolte.house.gov](https://obernolte.house.gov/media/press-releases/obernolte-trahan-release-discussion-draft-great-american-ai-act) · [Roll Call](https://rollcall.com/2026/06/04/bipartisan-ai-draft-proposes-three-year-preemption-of-state-laws/) · [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/317903/20260606/federal-ai-regulation-bill-freezes-state-consumer-protections-three-years-sparks-revolt.htm) · **Type:** policy · **Time (UTC):** Jun 4

Reps. Jay Obernolte (R-CA) and Lori Trahan (D-MA) released a 269-page discussion draft of the Great American AI Act on June 4 — the first bipartisan federal framework with substantive frontier-developer requirements. Key provisions: a three-year freeze on new state laws governing AI model development (training, weighting, architecture); mandatory semi-annual audits by CAISI-licensed third parties for "large frontier developers" (companies with $500M+ annual revenue); a 15-day critical-safety-incident reporting window (24 hours if there is imminent danger); civil penalties up to $1 million per day for non-compliance; and a $100M/year Center for AI Safety and Innovation at NIST. The bill preserves state authority over AI deployment and use, and leaves existing civil rights, labor, privacy, and copyright protections intact.

Notable casualties of the preemption clause include California AB 2013 (training-data disclosure) and portions of SB 942 (AI watermarking). Opposition organized within hours: the AFL-CIO called the draft a "hard no," Public Citizen labelled it "disastrous," and the House AI Commission formally rejected it as inadequate. Tech industry groups broadly supported the measure.

**Why it matters:** The Great American AI Act is the most substantive federal AI governance draft to date; if passed, it would nullify roughly two dozen active state AI bills during the period Colorado's AI Act takes effect (June 30). The core tension — federal preemption vs. state consumer protection — positions the bill as the main legislative battleground for AI governance in the 119th Congress.

## SpaceX IPO roadshow opens at fixed $135 per share <a id="spacex-ipo-roadshow"></a>

**Source:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/03/spacex-ipo-stock-price-roadshow-musk.html) · [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/317793/20260604/spacex-ipo-roadshow-launches-fixed-price-wall-street-says-stock-worth-half-ask.htm) · **Type:** funding · **Time (UTC):** Jun 4

SpaceX launched its IPO roadshow on June 4, offering 556.6 million shares at a fixed price of $135 — an unusual structure for a listing of this scale that forgoes the traditional bookbuilding price range. The implied valuation is $1.75 trillion, targeting $75 billion in gross proceeds, which would be the largest IPO in stock market history. Share pricing is scheduled after market close on June 11; first trading day is June 12 on Nasdaq under ticker SPCX. SpaceX's 2025 revenue was $18.7 billion post-xAI merger. Wall Street analysts cited in the TechTimes piece value the shares at $60–80, roughly half the offering price.

**Why it matters:** SpaceX is Anthropic's largest new compute supplier (Colossus 1, $1.25B/month contract), so its public-market capitalization directly affects the AI infrastructure supply chain economics. The $1.75T valuation also crystallizes the market's current appetite for AI-infrastructure bets at a moment when Anthropic is preparing its own IPO.

## Arizona Public Service proposes 45% rate hike for data centers <a id="arizona-aps-rate-hike"></a>

**Source:** [Cryptobriefing](https://cryptobriefing.com/arizona-aps-rate-hike-data-centers/) · [AZ Capitol Times](https://azcapitoltimes.com/news/2026/03/10/who-pays-for-arizonas-ai-power-boom/) · **Type:** policy · **Time (UTC):** —

Arizona Public Service (APS) filed a rate case with the Arizona Corporation Commission requesting a $662M revenue increase — a 45%+ hike for extra-large electricity users including data centers, alongside a 14–16% residential increase (roughly $20/month). APS forecasts peak load growing by up to 40% by 2031, with AI data centers as the primary driver; the utility argues data centers must contribute proportionately to the new grid infrastructure they require. A final ACC decision is expected before end of 2026.

**Why it matters:** Arizona is home to major AI data center clusters for Microsoft, Meta, and Google. A 45% electricity surcharge on top of current rates — before a final decision — is already shifting some operators' site-selection calculus; if the ACC approves the full request, it will be the first jurisdiction to specifically price AI data center load at a material premium, a template other utilities are watching closely.
