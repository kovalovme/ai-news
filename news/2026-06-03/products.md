## Anthropic expands Project Glasswing to 150 additional organizations <a id="project-glasswing-expansion"></a>

**Source:** [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/expanding-project-glasswing) · [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/02/anthropic-scales-claude-mythos-to-critical-infrastructure-in-15-countries/) · **Type:** launch · **Time (UTC):** Jun 2

Anthropic added approximately 150 new partners across 15+ countries to Project Glasswing, its initiative providing access to Claude Mythos Preview for automated vulnerability scanning and patch development. The expansion adds sectors underrepresented in the initial 50-partner April cohort: power, water, healthcare, communications, and hardware, with most new partners operating codebases that could affect more than 100 million people in a major attack. Current high-profile partners include Apple, Nvidia, Microsoft, CrowdStrike, and Palo Alto Networks.

Since launch, Glasswing partners have collectively identified more than 10,000 high- or critical-severity software flaws. Claude Mythos Preview goes beyond detection: it can write working exploits, chain multiple vulnerabilities together, and suggest patches — capabilities Anthropic describes as "surpass[ing] all but the most skilled humans" at finding and exploiting software weaknesses.

Anthropic explicitly declined to commit to a general-availability timeline for Mythos, citing the absence of sufficiently robust misuse safeguards: "we'll need highly robust safeguards that prevent the model's cyber capabilities from being misused — safeguards that we (and, to our knowledge, all other AI developers) have yet to develop."

**Why it matters:** Project Glasswing is the only public-facing deployment of a frontier model deliberately withheld from general release for capability-safety reasons. The 10,000-flaw metric and 200-org total give the first concrete scale signal for what a specialized cyber-AI can find at enterprise scope; the explicit acknowledgment of missing safeguards is a rare candid public statement on the gap between current capability and safe deployment.
