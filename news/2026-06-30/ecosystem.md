## Google loses multiple senior Gemini researchers to Anthropic and OpenAI <a id="google-talent-exodus"></a>

**Source:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-24/google-poised-to-lose-two-more-high-profile-ai-staffers-to-anthropic) · [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/24/ai-researchers-continue-to-leave-google-for-its-rivals/) · [Inc.](https://www.inc.com/moses-jeanfrancois/why-google-just-lost-4-key-staffers-to-anthropic-and-openai/91365820) · **Type:** update · **Time (UTC):** Jun 21–27

Between June 21 and June 27 — the same week Gemini 3.5 Pro missed its GA commitment — at least five senior Gemini researchers announced departures. Jonas Adler (AI coding effort lead) and Alexander Pritzel (pretraining) are joining Anthropic, as reported by Bloomberg on June 24. They join Nobel laureate John Jumper and AI-safety researcher Arthur Conmy, who also departed for Anthropic in the same window; prominent researcher Noam Shazeer moved to OpenAI. Anthropic's $965B post-money valuation from its May Series H and the prospect of pre-IPO equity are cited across reporting as the primary draw.

**Why it matters:** Losing pretraining and coding-effort leadership simultaneously, concurrent with a public GA miss, suggests deeper execution pressure on Gemini 3.5 Pro than the stated "token-efficiency testing" explanation implies; the pattern has now repeated across two high-profile departure clusters in 2026.

## Fable 5 Day 18: status unchanged <a id="fable5-day18"></a>

**Source:** [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/fable-mythos-access) · **Type:** update · **Time (UTC):** ongoing
